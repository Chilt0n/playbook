P1: KeepKey Error Connecting on Platform / not detected (Mac or Win)

Issue Explanation

User’s KeepKey is not detected by their Mac/Win machine. That means on the Platform it says “disconnected” in the top right hand corner or “device not detected” in the pop up. [need image of this]

Follow these steps:

  1. P1 - T1: Refresh and try to Connect Wallet again see P20 [link]
  2. P1 - T2: Make sure beta.shapeshift.com isn’t open in +1 tabs/windows or the KeepKey client isn’t open (device will not be detected if it’s connected elsewhere) or updater app
  3. P1 - T3: Forget > Refresh > Re-connect Device see P18 [link]
  4. P1 - T4: Try A Different USB Port
    • A. Unplug your KeepKey cable from your USB port (make sure KeepKey device is still connected to the other end of the cable).
    • B. Plug usb into different USB port.
  5. P1 - T5: Try a completely new micro-usb cable (we do not sell cable separately).
  6. P1 - T6:Clear Cache/History > Refresh > Try P1-T1 again.
    • A. In the Chrome browser, click ‘History’ in the top left side of the screen.
    • B. Click ‘Show Full History’.
    • C. On the left side you will see a button to click called ‘Clear Browsing Data’.
    • D. Click ‘Clear Data’ and your history/cache will be cleared.
  7. P1 - T7: Firmware Upgrade via Updater App see P16 [link] Platform Updater App Download-
    • Windows: http://beta.shapeshift.com/keepkey-updater-pc.exe
    • Mac: http://beta.shapeshift.com/keepkey-updater-mac.dmg
  8. P1 - T8: Firmware upgrade in KeepKey client if updater app doesn’t work, see KK6 [link]
  9. P1 - T9: Request logs from user so our developers can look further into the issue. (helpdesk on requesting logs)
    • P1-T10A: Re-create the error on beta.shapeshift.com
    • P1-T10B: Right click with your mouse and click inspect
    • P1-T10C: Click ‘Console’
    • P1-T10D: Right-click the code and select ‘Save As’ to save file to your computer.
    • P1-T10E: Request the user reply to the email where logs were requested, and attach logs.
    • P1-T10F: Once logs are received, Assignee > Escalations and leave an internal note that logs need to be delivered to dev team.
    • P1-T10G: In - house team should include answers to the following questions when shipping logs to the dev team:
      • 1) what OS are they using? have they tried other OSes / machines?
      • 2) if it's windows, have you had them try the zadig workaround?
      • 3) if it's linux, are their udev rules up to date?
      • 4) what firmware version do they have on their device?
      • 5) what are the symptoms? (I can tell from these logs, and you should be able to as well by now, that the device didn't connect, and isn't communicating with the host).... what else do you know about what's going on?
  10. P1 - T11: If unable to assist customer with the above, escalate to: change Assignee > Escalations and leave internal note with details on issue.

Post-troubleshooting Steps

  • Step 1: Tag support ticket with CODE [ code = Issue # + Step you solved the problem with in the troubleshooting list, example: P1T2 (platform issue 1, troubleshooting step 2). If you can not solve with any of the above steps, tag as PXXNONE.
